Detailed Description

Range Info message structure.

This message is sent by the local requesting Radio to its Host at the end of a full UWB ranging conversation or timeout. Timeouts, indicated in the status field, can occur if the targeted responder failed to receive, or the requester failed to acquire the response packet.

◆ rangeStatus

rcrm_uint8_t rcmMsg_FullRangeInfo::rangeStatus

Status of the range conversation. Even-numbered codes may be combined in certain circumstances, and function as a bitmask. Odd-numbered codes (LSB set) are unique error codes and are not combined with other error codes.

• 0 - Success: range measurement was completed with no issues.

• 1 - Timeout - never received the range response

• 3 - Virtual Carrier Sense - indicates that another ongoing range conversation would have been interrupted by our range attempt, so no range measurement was attempted.

• 5, 7, 9, 11, 13, 15 are all reserved, internal error codes

• 17 - CRC Mismatch - the UWB range response packet was received at the expected time, but failed its CRC check.

• 19 - reserved, internal error code

Completed range conversations where a measurement issue was detected might have one or more of the following bits set.

• 2 (Bit 1) - Requester LED Failure - Indicates the requesting radio could not determine the exact timing of the pulse for range measurement. • 4 (Bit 2) - Responder LED Failure - Indicates the responding radio could not determine the exact timing of the pulse for range measurement.

• 32 (Bit 5) - Range Out of Bounds - Indicates the range calculation produced a bogus result.

◆ antennaMode

rcrm_uint8_t rcmMsg_FullRangeInfo::antennaMode

Antenna Mode. Sets the antenna used for TX and RX.

• 0 = A[TX/RX]

• 1 = B[TX/RX]

• 2 = A[TX]B[RX]

• 3 = A[RX]B[TX])

In addition, setting the high order bit (0x80) of this byte enables automatic toggling of the antenna for each superframe (even-numbered superframes use the “base” antenna mode, and odd-numbered superframes use the inverse antenna mode)

Note
Default value is 0(0x00)

◆ noise

rcrm_uint16_t rcmMsg_FullRangeInfo::noise

Noise represents the noise generated in the receiver from the received waveform scan. Along with Vpeak, Noise can be used to compute SNR using 20 * Log10 (Vpeak / Noise). Due to processing constraints in the radio CPU, this noise is an estimate based on a partial rather than full analysis of the waveform scan.
